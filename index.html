Jieun Yoo

Hi. I completed my PhD in May 2025 at Univ. Illinois Chicago (UIC) in the field of experimental particle physics. I'm interested in searches for new physics, and in developing the next generation of detectors that will help us push the bounds of our knowledge of the physical world.

As a member of the CMS collaboration, one of the largest scientific experiments at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), I developed expertise in machine learning, precision Higgs boson physics, and silicon detectors. Within the Smart Pixels collaboration, I helped launch a hardware-software co-designed neural network implemented on an ASIC chip to address the challenge of large data volumes at higher luminosity and to enable new physics searches.

Previously, I was a member of the T2K and CAPTAIN collaborations, both neutrino experiments. I contributed to detector studies for the T2K Pi-Zero Detector (P0D) in Tokai, Japan, and participated in the first neutron beam runs using the CAPTAIN liquid argon time projection chamber (LArTPC) installed in the beamline at Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL).
